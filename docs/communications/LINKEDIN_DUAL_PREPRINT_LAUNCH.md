# LinkedIn launch: evidence-typed governance and fail-closed AI services

## Publish-ready post

AI systems do not become trustworthy because a build passes. They become governable when claims, evidence, approvals, execution, and outcomes remain distinguishable and auditable.

I have released two 24-page public preprints that examine that boundary from complementary directions. Both records are PREPRINTS; NOT PEER REVIEWED.

1. *From Build Success to Admissible Proof: Evidence-Typed Governance for a Mixed Lean and Executable Formula Corpus*

DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21332317

GitHub release: https://github.com/szl-holdings/evidence-typed-formula-governance/releases/tag/v0.1.0

2. *Readiness Is Not Evidence: Fail-Closed Epistemic Boundaries for Governed AI Services*

DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21332338

GitHub release: https://github.com/szl-holdings/fail-closed-governed-ai-services/releases/tag/v0.1.0

The repository behind this research direction already includes the SZL Lake, Lean/mathlib-linked formula governance, sovereign inference gates, execution receipts, and a Brain catalog containing 9,464 raw nodes.

That inventory is not presented as model qualification. All 9,464 raw Brain nodes remain quarantined from training with `training_eligible=false`. The M1 model candidate remains NOT_PROMOTED and its quality remains NOT_ESTABLISHED. ReceiptAgent is currently a release contract, not a promoted model; ReceiptAgent-specific weights have not been released.

That boundary is deliberate: measured results remain MEASURED, unverified capabilities remain BLOCKED, and missing evidence does not become a green status.

For CTOs and defense or government engineering teams, the practical question is not merely whether an AI system can produce an answer. It is whether the system can show what evidence it used, which constraints passed, when it abstained, who authorized execution, and what happened afterward.

That is the engineering direction: proof-carrying decision infrastructure with fail-closed controls and auditable receipts.
