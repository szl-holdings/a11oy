a11oy Restraint Doctrine v11

Before the a11oy Code agent emits a diff, it descends a 6-rung ladder and stops at the first rung that holds — (1) YAGNI, (2) stdlib, (3) native platform feature, (4) already-installed dependency, (5) one line, then (6) minimum viable code. Deliberate simplifications are marked with restraint: ceiling comments that name the upgrade path. Every decision becomes a signed DSSE receipt and an advisory Λ trust score; the benchmark reproduces code-reduction / cost / speed numbers on our stack, labelled MEASURED only when actually run, else SAMPLE/ROADMAP.

Provenance — honest  The ladder and the lite / full / ultra intensity levels are adopted from the open-source Ponytail coding-agent skill (github.com/DietrichGebert/ponytail, MIT, © 2026 DietrichGebert) — adopted + governed, not invented here. Our additions are the signed receipts, the Λ score, the measured-on-our-stack benchmark, and the energy tie-in. Ponytail's published numbers (80–94% less code, 47–77% cheaper, 3–6× faster, median across Haiku/Sonnet/Opus) are cited as Ponytail's, never claimed as ours. Λ uniqueness is Conjecture 1 (OPEN), advisory floor < 1.0 — not a proven theorem.
