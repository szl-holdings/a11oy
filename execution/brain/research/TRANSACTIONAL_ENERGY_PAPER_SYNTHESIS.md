# Transactional energy paper synthesis

Paper: Lloyd Watts and Carver Mead, "A Wave-Particle Model of Energy Transfer Between
Two Atoms in a Transactional Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics," Entropy 2026,
28, 813. DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/e28070813. License shown in the paper: CC BY 4.0.

## What the paper proposes

The authors propose an analytic model in which in-transit photon energy density is
proportional to an overlap of retarded emitter and advanced absorber four-vector
potentials. Their product construction appears in Equation 33; later RMS integration
defines total energy. Conservation produces a transition-time relationship proportional
to the square root of emitter-absorber distance, and a constant is calibrated by matching
an absorber-weighted mean to a hydrogen Lyman-alpha lifetime from Fermi's Golden Rule.

The work is analytic and numerical. It reports no new hardware experiment or direct
measurement. The authors explicitly leave single/double-slit cases, EPR, HBT, and the
Freedman-Clauser experiment for future work. SZL therefore records it as a proposed
physics model, not independently established theory.

## Defensible engineering inspiration

- two-sided proposal and confirmation before an action is committed;
- overlap or RMS fusion as a heuristic to test against normal baselines;
- conservation-style reconciliation across submitted, accepted, rejected, and failed work;
- latency-aware calibration over measured distances or dependency depth;
- phase/coherence visualizations that remain MODELED until benchmarks validate them.

The paper does not evidence quantum computation, a quantum speedup, faster-than-light
communication, a trained AI model, or a fully operational brain.
