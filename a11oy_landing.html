The governed loop

Gate before it runs. Sign what it did.

Most AI stacks bolt safety on after the answer. a11oy puts a deny-by-default governor in front of every action and a signed, hash-chained receipt behind it — so a buyer, an auditor, or a regulator can replay the decision instead of trusting a screenshot.

⛓

Gate

Each turn clears the constitution, the doctrine gate, and the policy guards before a single token is generated. No clearance, no run.

⚖

Route

Smart Routing picks the smallest sovereign engine that clears the sensitivity class and license tier — self-hosted first, never leaking sensitive context.

🔏

Sign

The decision is sealed as a DSSE receipt, ECDSA‑P256 signed and hash-chained (SHA3‑256). Signed on write, never on a read.

What gets checked

1 · POST /api/a11oy/v1/govern/infer — a real governed turn.
2 · GET /cosign.pub — the in-image public key (PEM).
3 · Rebuild the DSSE PAE, recompute SHA‑256, run crypto.subtle.verify.

DER→raw r‖s conversion happens in-browser; the private key never leaves the box.

Why it matters

A signature anyone can check, against a key they fetched themselves, is the difference between “trust us” and proof. The same logic backs every receipt in the ledger.
